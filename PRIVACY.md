## Overview

Whence the Rave? is an independent event discovery app. This policy explains what information the app uses, how it is handled, and your choices.

We do **not** sell your data, use third-party advertising trackers, or build advertising profiles.

---

## Information the app uses

### Location (when in use)

If you grant permission, the app uses your device location **only while the app is in use** to:

- Show your position on the map
- Find the nearest supported search area
- Calculate distance to event venues
- Support follow mode and compass heading on the map

Location data is processed on your device and is **not** transmitted to our servers. We do not store your location history on external servers.

You can deny or revoke location access at any time in iOS Settings.

### Notifications (optional)

If you choose to set an event reminder, the app may request notification permission. Reminders are **local notifications** scheduled on your device. We do not use push notifications or remote notification servers.

You control which events get reminders and can remove them from the app or iOS Settings.

### Local storage on your device

The app caches event listings, area databases, and your preferences (such as map settings) on your device to improve performance and support offline use. You can clear cached data in the app Settings.

### Network requests

The app fetches publicly available event listings and related metadata from [ra.co](https://ra.co) (Resident Advisor). Requests include search parameters such as area and date — not personal account credentials, because the app does not require you to sign in.

## What we do not collect

- No user accounts or login within the app
- No third-party analytics or advertising SDKs
- No sale or sharing of location data for marketing
- No collection of contacts, photos, or health data
